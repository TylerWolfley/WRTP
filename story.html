Story

A place defined by store, gallery, and road

Wind River Trading Post is shaped as much by its setting as by the things inside it. On Highway 287 in Fort Washakie, the trading post and the Gallery of the Wind share one roof and create a stop that feels more layered than a standard retail storefront.

A place on Highway 287

The location matters. Wind River Trading Post is not presented like a tucked-away boutique or a polished lifestyle brand. It sits directly in the path of travel through Fort Washakie, which gives the place a plain, real presence.

Trading post and gallery

What makes the site worth building carefully is the pairing inside the building itself. One side is a trading post built around jewelry, beadwork, clothing, art, and regional goods. The other is the Gallery of the Wind, which adds artifacts and artwork connected to the Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho peoples.

Fort Washakie

Fort Washakie gives the place its context. The site should feel tied to that setting rather than floating above it, which is why the writing stays grounded and the design avoids generic western or tourism language.
