Wind River Trading Post sits on Highway 287 in Fort Washakie, where a trading post and the Gallery of the Wind share the same building on the Wind River Indian Reservation. The store carries jewelry, beadwork, clothing, art, and regional goods, while the museum space adds artifacts and artwork connected to the Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho peoples.

Trading Post

Jewelry, beadwork, clothing, and regional goods

The store is a real trading post, not a gift-shop category list. Jewelry, beadwork, clothing, art, and practical goods sit together in one space, with inventory that changes over time rather than staying fixed like a catalog.

Gallery of the Wind

Artifacts and artwork under the same roof

Inside the same building, the Gallery of the Wind gives the place more depth than a standard retail stop. The museum space holds artifacts and artwork connected to the Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho peoples, making the visit feel grounded in place rather than purely commercial.
